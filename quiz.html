1.Does a truck always have more momentum than a bike??

2.What's the momentum of a 30kg doge running towards south at 2m/s?

3.What happens when a 2kg ball moving 3m/s east towards a stationary ball with the same weight? Ignoring air resistance and friction.

4.When do you have momentum?

5.What happens after the collision of a 1kg ball is moving 2/s east comes into perfect contact with another 1kg ball moving west? Again, ignoring air resistance and friction.

Question 1: The correct answer is No, a moving bike will have more momentum than a stationary truck.

Question 2: The correct answer is 60kgm/s because p = m * v, 30kg * 2m/s = 60kgm/s.

Question 3: The correct answer is Conservation of momentum will happen. The stationary ball will move towards east at 3m/s after collision.

Question 4: The correct answer is When you are running you have velocity and momentum.

Question 5: The correct answer is two balls will moves opposite direction at the same speed.
