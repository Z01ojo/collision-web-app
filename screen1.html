What is mass?

Every object is made up of matter. The more matter an object has, means the more mass it has. Mass is usually measure in units of kilogram, kg. For instance, you have mass and your mass might be 55kg. Keep in mind that your weight is not entirely depends on mass but also gravity. Weight is how much gravity pulls on mass. Fun Fact, the Earth is about 5973600000000000000000000 kg.

Mass or m = kg

What is velocity?

Velocity simply put is the vector quantity measure of the speed of the object in a specific direction. For example, the dog is moving at 1 meter/second north.

Velocity = distance/time

v = d/t or m/s

What is momentum?

Momentum is a measurement of mass in motion. Any object that is moving has momentum. In physics, momentum of an object is equal to the mass times the velocity.

Momentum = Mass * Velocity

p = m * v or kgm/s
