There are 3 main types of collisions


Inelastic collision: The kinetic energy(KE) is not conserved.
Example- When a bullet hitting a block of wood.

Perfectly inelastic collision: Two objects stick together after a collsion. P is conserved, but not KE.
Example - two clumps of pizza dough.

Elastic collision: Two objects does not stick after collision.P and KE are conserved.
Example - two billiard balls hitting each other.
